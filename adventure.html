Rome

A New Year's Eve rock concert at Colosseum was not one to forget. A city dwelling on the memory of men can still radiates young energy, making me question what I think I know.

Venice

I expected to be blown away by the beauty of Venice I read so much in literature, but it was not the case. A city so unique was just another victim of consumerism. Ponte dei Sospiri was wrapped around in ads, gondola became an over-priced yet inefficient way of transportation, and the overall lack originality in the islands of Murano and Burano make me realize fame smears instead of sublimating.

Istanbul

Getting to Istanbul was an adventure by itself. After a 4-hour bus ride from Boston to New York and a 10-hour flight from JFK to Istanbul, I had only 19 hours to absorb what the great Constantinople has to offer before catching another 13-hour flight to Osaka. Absorbing the great beauty of Constantinople in just one day made me realize the fleeting nature of beauty.

Shangri-La

My way of exploring the mystery land was to sketch every piece of artifact, garment, and paintings I found interesting in Shangri-La's exotic cafe, guest house, mountains, and villages. Then I added notes on my sketches after I did some research. The sketch book is now my scared guide to Shangri-La.
